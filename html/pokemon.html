Pokemon is a series of video games developed by Game Freak and published by Nintendo and The Pokemon Company. The games are role-playing games (RPGs) with an element of strategy, and were first released in 1996 in Japan. The games are set in a world inhabited by creatures called Pokemon, which can be captured, trained, and used to battle other trainers. The series has since expanded to include trading card games, an animated television series, movies, and merchandise.


The main series of Pokemon games are released in pairs, with each pair featuring slightly different Pokemon that can be caught in the game. Players are encouraged to trade Pokemon with other players to complete their collection. The games are known for their simple gameplay mechanics, colorful graphics, and catchy music.

There are currently eight generations of Pokemon games, with the most recent generation being released in 2019. The games have sold over 300 million copies worldwide, making them one of the best-selling video game franchises of all time.
